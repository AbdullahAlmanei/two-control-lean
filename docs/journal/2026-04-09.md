# Daily log

## Goal
Move past the Section 3 cleanup and finish the Section 4 Lean development, especially Lemmas 4.3 and 4.4, while getting the file out of the timeout-heavy state and back to a clean build.

## What I changed
Implemented the full proof of Lemma 4.3 in `TwoControl/Section4.lean` using a block decomposition of `V₃`, elimination of the off-diagonal blocks, extraction of the surviving 2×2 unitary relation, and reduction back to `section3_lemma_3_3`. Then completed Lemma 4.4 by reducing it to Lemma 4.3 through conjugation by `swapab`, adjoints, and starred parameters. Along the way I built a substantial Section-4-local helper layer for block/unitary reasoning, tensor reassociation, and swap conjugation. The important late refactor was to replace the earlier brute-force 8×8 finite-case proofs with structural tensor-product lemmas for `swap2`, `swapab`, `acgate`, `bcgate`, and `ccu`. After that rewrite, `lake build TwoControl.Section4` succeeded, and the full project `lake build` also succeeded.

## What worked
The mathematical proof plans for both lemmas were correct; the real issue was proof shape, not theorem statements. For Lemma 4.3, the block-matrix route was stable once the helper lemmas around `blockify`, diagonal unitary blocks, and the `acgate` tensor action were in place. For Lemma 4.4, the symmetry argument through `swapab` and adjoints worked once the swap/conjugation layer stopped trying to prove everything by raw matrix expansion. Reusing the diagonalization and Kronecker helpers extracted on April 8 also paid off directly.

## What failed
Several intermediate proofs were too expensive for Lean to elaborate reliably. In particular, the first versions of `acgate_kron_two`, `swapab_conj_acgate`, `swapab_conj_bcgate`, and `swapab_conj_ccu_diag2` repeatedly hit deterministic heartbeat timeouts because they expanded large tensor expressions and 8×8 matrix products too aggressively. One detour through `Matrix.swap` also turned out to be brittle and ran into classical `decide` issues, so it was discarded in favor of direct structural lemmas. There were also a few rounds where editor diagnostics looked cleaner than the full `lake build`, so some failures had to be isolated from fresh build logs rather than from the editor alone.

## Reusable lesson
When tensor/permutation proofs start timing out, raising `maxHeartbeats` is usually not the real fix. The better move is to stop doing giant finite-case matrix expansions and instead prove small reindexing, associativity, and conjugation lemmas that expose the tensor structure directly. Once the proof is phrased in those structural terms, the build usually becomes both faster and more stable.
