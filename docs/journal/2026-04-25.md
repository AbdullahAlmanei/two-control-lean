# Daily log

## Goal
Finish the Clifford Lemma 11 Lean track by completing the one-qubit synthesis layer, proving the final two-qubit synthesis theorem, and aligning the blueprint with the theorem shape that the formalization actually supports.

## What I changed
Completed the proof stack in `TwoControl/Clifford/Statements.lean`. The first major step was finishing `one_qubit_exact_h_t_rz`, which synthesizes every one-qubit unitary over `{H, T, R_z(theta)}` up to global phase. The proof prepares the first column with an `R_z R_y` circuit, diagonalizes the remaining unitary, and then absorbs the diagonal into a global phase times a final `R_z`.

After that, I proved the final Lemma 11 theorem as a global-phase synthesis result. The theorem now states that every two-qubit unitary has a circuit over the target primitives whose matrix is `GlobalPhaseEquivalent` to the unitary. The proof applies the existing cosine-sine decomposition, synthesizes the two block-diagonal side factors using the demultiplexing lemma plus one-qubit synthesis, synthesizes the middle conditional-`R_y` factor via Lemma 3 and the controlled-`R_z` circuit, and then composes the resulting circuit lists.

To make the final proof manageable, I added a small `GlobalPhaseEquivalent` API for exact equality, reflexivity, transitivity, multiplication, and lifting phases through `localOnFirst` and `localOnSecond`. I also added private helper witnesses for the exact conditional-`R_y` circuit and the up-to-phase block-diagonal side-factor circuits. This kept the final Lemma 11 proof short and mostly about assembling already-proved pieces.

The completed theorem intentionally uses global phase rather than exact equality. That matches the repaired one-qubit theorem and avoids pretending that the accumulated scalar phases disappear for free under the current centered `R_z` semantics.

## What worked
The cleanest move was to make global phase explicit and then prove small transport lemmas for it. Once scalar phases could be composed and lifted through local gates, the final two-qubit proof became a direct assembly of cosine-sine decomposition, demultiplexing, controlled-`R_z` synthesis, and one-qubit synthesis.

The exact `T`-power identities for `S` and `S^\dagger` also paid off. They avoided a fragile scalar-to-`R_z` detour and made the `R_y` circuitization layer much easier to use in the final proof.

## What failed
The earlier exact theorem shape was too strong for the available one-qubit synthesis result. Trying to keep exact equality would have required an additional phase-elimination theorem showing that any global scalar can itself be realized exactly as a circuit in the target gate set. That is a separate mathematical obligation, not a proof-script cleanup.

There were also a few local Lean proof issues while composing the final circuit: most were orientation and associativity problems around `twoQubitCircuitMatrix_append`, Kronecker scalar transport, and redundant `simp` calls after rewrites had already closed the goal.

## Reusable lesson
When a proof naturally produces equality up to global phase, formalize that relation early and give it the same basic algebraic interface as equality. In quantum circuit synthesis, this prevents scalar bookkeeping from leaking into every matrix calculation and makes the final composition proof much closer to the paper argument.
