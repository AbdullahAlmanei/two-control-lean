# Daily log

## Goal
Stabilize and simplify the Section 3 Lean development by cleaning warnings, reducing file clutter, and extracting the most clearly reusable helper layer without changing theorem statements.

## What I changed
Cleaned the deprecation and linter warnings in `TwoControl/Section3.lean`, then did a conservative refactor by moving the reusable 2×2 diagonalization and tensor-product helper lemmas into a new file, `TwoControl/DiagonalizationHelpers.lean`. I kept the Section 3 theorem statements unchanged, updated the imports, and rebuilt the target to confirm the split was still sound.

## What worked
The conservative extraction boundary was the right one: the generic diagonalization and Kronecker-product lemmas moved cleanly into their own module, while the more Section-3-specific controlled-gate and finite-case helper lemmas stayed local. After rebuilding, `TwoControl.Section3` compiled successfully and the editor diagnostics were clean.

## What failed
The first pass at the refactor paused too early at the planning stage, and one build was launched from the wrong directory before being rerun from the Lean project root. There was also a short-lived stale-import message in the editor until the module was rebuilt.

## Reusable lesson
For formalization refactors, it is better to extract only the helper layer that is obviously general and already reused, then validate immediately with a focused rebuild. Pulling theorem-local case splits and witness constructions into shared modules too early usually makes the structure worse, not better.
