## What worked
The important pattern across both sections was to expose the right structure early instead of trying to simplify everything at once. For Section 5, the proof became stable once the middle product was expressed as explicit block data and the forward direction was reduced to tensor-factor statements that matched the Section 4 interface. For Section 6, the high-level plan for 6.4 was correct once it was broken into front-end normalization, identity transfer, and branch-specific reduction. In both files, the most reliable proofs were the ones written as short `calc` blocks with explicit insertions of unitary identities and helper lemmas tailored to the exact tensor or projector pattern in the goal.

## What failed
Several early proof attempts were too brittle. In Section 5, direct manipulation of the full 8-by-8 equality did not expose the diagonal information cleanly enough, so the argument only settled once it was rewritten around block decompositions and custom helper lemmas instead of broad simplification. In Section 6, broad `simp` and `change` steps that looked acceptable in the editor were unstable under `lake build`, and one normalizer block even turned into a parser and indentation failure after a bad closing step. The first version of the 6.4 special-word algebra also left a build-only unsolved goal because generic simplification did not clear the many `⊗ 0` terms and nested `proj0`/`proj1` chains. A couple of helper proofs also became heartbeat-heavy when they went through `isQubit_of_kron_right`, so I replaced those with direct dot-product arguments.

## Reusable lesson
For these tensor-control proofs, the real bottleneck is almost never the theorem statement; it is whether the proof exposes the right intermediate structure. When a proof mixes controlled gates, block matrices, and Kronecker products, it is usually better to add narrowly targeted rewrite lemmas and move through an interface theorem like the Section 4 criteria than to enlarge a global `simp` set or push harder on finite expansion. Editor diagnostics are also not a sufficient check on their own; the final arbiter is a fresh `lake build`.
